Error Detector

This detector is designed primarily for writers of scientific articles, particularly the fields of information and computer science. Use a generic grammar detector first (e.g. Grammarly ). This detector is coded to find mistakes that occur in a corpus of computer and information science research articles. If you would like to see how this tool works, click one-minute tutorial.

Accuracy detector

A1 Factual errors A2 Overgeneralization errors A3 Overly bold claims A4 Numerical or statistical errors A5 Numerous grammatical, lexical and spelling errors

Brevity detector

B1 Multiple vague words B2 Repetition B3 Redundancy

Clarity detector

C1 Vague terms C2 Ambiguous terms C3 Ambiguous references C4 Ambiguous syntax C5 Garden-path sentences

Objectivity detector

O1 Feeling/People focus O2 Emotive O3 Excessive personalisation

Formality detector

F1 Contraction using apostrophes F2 Abbreviation issues F3 Slang F4 Informal quantifiers adverbs, format and linking F5 Rhetorical questions

Test items for accuracy:A5 Noun phrases: uncountable nouns e.g. accommodations, clothings, furnitures, homeworks, informations, knowledges, lightnings, luggages, musics, publics, researches, slangs, trainings, transportations, works); irregular plural nouns +..s, (e.g. datas, each data, two data); determiners and quantifiers, such as one of the + plural, each + plural (e.g. each features), a hour, another + plural (e.g. another articles), much + plural (e.g. much helps), many + no plural (e.g. many time); Prepositional phrases: despite followed by clause (e.g. despite there is a problem); A5 Verb phrases: Intransitive verbs (e.g. be occurred, be happened, There happened, There occurred), improbable combinations (was did, I am belonging, modal verbs followed by -ed (e.g. may worked), can to);A5 Linking: moreover, errant comma before although, Also ; A5 averagely, more + ...er (e.g. more quicker); this paper concludes that, if ;A5 Accidentally repeated words (e.g. and and); A5 Collocation - independent mutually, as follow ; A5 Part of speech errors: (e.g. I analysis, They analysis, I am belong); A5 Careless spelling mistakes (e.g. seve, mesured, randam)

Test items for brevity: and so on, and so forth, etc, etcetera, such as in day by day, little by little, step by step,discuss about, each and every, general public, I think, I do not think, join together, see it with your eyes

Test items for clarity: thing, something, someone, somebody, you, researchers say, it is said that, a very long timegood, bad, nice, really, get, got, gotten, you, your, yours, he, she, they, them

Test items for objectivity: excellent, perfect, fabulous, fantastic, at last, eventually, unfortunately, you, your, yourself

Test items for formality: it's, undefined abbreviations, some phrasal verbs like kicked out, lots of, a lot of, plenty of, really, right after, straight after, right before, yet (e.g. has not been completed yet) straight before, Japanese font apostrophe (e.g didn`t), And, So, But
